## Finished: Where the Red Fern Grows

ISBN: 0-553-27429-5

The first book (finally finished - thanks thunderstorm!) I decided to read for the year. You know, resolutions and all that, reading among them. Housemate learned of my resolution, and suggested one from their library that they were looking to donate. I took the challenge of reading just one chapter per session, but yesterday (2025-05-10) we had a few isolated cells rumble through, so I wasn't on the computer playing video games as a distraction to complete resolutions or other improvement objectives.

Two parts of the book captured my attention: working 2 years for $50, and the ending (spoiler: there's an ending! :smiling_imp:) kinda got me. No ugly crying, but tears did a-well. One or two may have escaped the lower eye prisons by way of an eye rub or something.

While I am sure it is meant for younger audiences (likely middle/junior or early high school), plus the religious references (something I don't ascribe to, but understand), I would recommend it for an afternoon read, if you are into the fiction side of things. I think it was a good tale, quite effective at describing the environments involved, no vulgarity I can remember, but that's a biased view because what others might consider vulgar, I might not. None of the "seven words you can't say on TV" kind of thing though.
